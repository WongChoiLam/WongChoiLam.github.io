### Abstract

Natural walking, one of many navigation techniques, is regarded as the most natural one with the greatest sense of presence. One problem faced by natural walking is the size limitation of physical space. Researchers have developed navigation techniques to enable natural walking in a larger virtual space. In addition, redirection techniques can be utilized to relax the space constraints. Another arduous problem of providing haptics feedbacks for VR experience also exists, some approaches to which share many similarities with redirection techniques. This article seeks to 1. present an overview of current available navigation techniques related to the problem of physical space constraint, redirection techniques and haptics retargeting techniques; 2. highlight their strenghs and limitations; 3. outline the connections between them.
